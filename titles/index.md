Connecting AMCAT scores, demographic and personal information to employment outcomes such as salary and job titles, we've done a comprehensive analysis of the labour market and the workforce in each profession. 

<div class="ui styled fluid accordion">
<div class="title active">
<i class="dropdown icon"></i>
Executive Summary
</div>
<div class="content active">
<p class="transition visible">
    <ul>
    <li> Systems Software Developers have the highest median salary ie Rs 3,70,000 whereas SEO Specialists are have the lowest median salary ie Rs 1,20,000.</li>
    <li> A lot workforces have the skill levels below the median supply point. Hiring in these professions needs to be investigated or it could mean the demand of professionals is much higher than the supply.</li>
    <li> There a few professions which have professionals more skillful than the job requires and similarly there are professions with supply less skillful than required by the job.</li>
    </ul>
</p>
</div>
</div>




<br>

##Where does each title stand?
<div class="ui styled fluid accordion">
    <div class="title active">
    <i class="dropdown icon"></i>How do titles rank based on skills?</div>
    <div class="content active">
    How does  software engineer rank against a business manager in English skills? Which workforces have high domain knowledge and how does the Civil and Mechanical engineering industry stand as compared to Computer Hardware and Networks in terms of compensation?
    </div>
    
    <div class="title">
    <i class="dropdown icon"></i>Highlights</div>
    <div class="content">
    <ul>
        <li>
        English | Business Analysts, Operations, Sales, Business Management etc are highers than supply etc. Other than software, all engineering roles are below the supply. Teaching professionals have the lowest English scores.
        </li>
        <li>
        Quantitative Ability | Analysts, Software, Core Engineering workforces have high Quant scores. Hardware, Teaching Professionals, Customer Representatives are below the supply.
        </li>
        <li>
        Logical Ability | All workforces other than Customer Representative are above the supply point.
        </li>
        <li>
        Degree Domain Knowledge | Teaching professional and Software jobforces have high scores in their domain of education in college.
        </li>
        <li>
        Job Domain Knowledge | Everyone is atleast above the supply.
        </li>
    </ul>
    </div>

</div>
